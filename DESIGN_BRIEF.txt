PULSE — DESIGN BRIEF
====================

Live site: https://pulse-poc-production.up.railway.app/
Admin table: https://pulse-poc-production.up.railway.app/admin/candidates
Repo: https://github.com/iancbradley83-arch/pulse-poc


1. WHAT PULSE IS
----------------

Pulse is a standalone, embeddable sports-content widget. Operators (sportsbooks
like Apuesta Total, Bet365, DraftKings, etc.) embed Pulse on their site to give
punters a news-driven feed of betting angles.

Every card on the feed is triggered by a real-world news event — an injury, a
team news story, a transfer, a manager quote, a tactical preview, a price move,
a goal scored. The card explains WHY a specific market (single, combo, or Bet
Builder) is interesting RIGHT NOW, and deep-links into the operator's bet slip.

It is NOT:
- A full sportsbook
- A pure odds board
- A stats-first calculator
- Operator-specific (it's content that plugs INTO operators)

It IS:
- A news-first content layer with markets as the action
- Mobile-first (but must render well inside iframe on desktop too)
- Themable (operators pass brand tokens: colors, font, radius, logo)
- Dark-mode native (sportsbook UIs skew dark — match that default)


2. TARGET USER
--------------

Casual-to-serious sports bettor browsing during the day, looking for reasons
to bet. Not a pro sharp. They want:
- A quick scan of what happened today that's bet-worthy
- A clear "here's the story, here's the bet" angle
- One-tap handoff to the operator bet slip
- Feeling like this is editorial curation, not a spammy feed


3. CURRENT STAGE
----------------

Stage 2 of the roadmap — news-driven recommendation engine + validation surface
is live. Cards are real:
- Fixtures come from Rogue API (Apuesta Total, ~1,800 soccer events catalogue)
- News comes from Claude Haiku 4.5 + web search per fixture
- Entity resolver maps mentions to fixtures, candidate builder picks markets,
  scorer + policy layer gates what reaches the feed

Two surfaces need design love:

  A. PUBLIC FEED (main consumer view)
     https://pulse-poc-production.up.railway.app/
     - Feed of pre-match cards
     - Mobile-phone-shell mock around it (Stage 5 removes the phone chrome
       when we ship the real embed widget)

  B. ADMIN /CANDIDATES TABLE (operator / editorial review)
     https://pulse-poc-production.up.railway.app/admin/candidates
     - Read-only table for humans to review what the engine produced,
       spot bad picks, tune thresholds
     - Desktop-first, dense


4. DESIGN PROBLEMS TO FIX (public feed)
---------------------------------------

Look at the live site. You'll see:

- Narrative body is a 6-line wall of text. No hierarchy. Should have a
  punchy headline + optional "read more" expand for the body.

- "Tonight's games" header is wrong — the cards cover fixtures over the next
  14 days, not just tonight.

- Filter chips at the top say "For You / NBA / NFL / EPL". Pulse is soccer-only
  for MVP. Chips should reflect what the content actually is: leagues
  (Premier League, La Liga, UCL, Serie A, Bundesliga), or hook types
  (Injuries, Team News, Transfers, Tactical), or both.

- Team avatars are flat 1-letter circles. Feels generic. Needs either real
  club badges (hard — licensing) or a more intentional typographic treatment
  (2–3 letter short code, team color accent).

- Hook-type pill (NEWS) is identical regardless of whether it's an injury,
  transfer, tactical story, etc. Needs differentiation — distinct color /
  icon per hook type (injury = red cross, team_news = team icon,
  transfer = arrow, tactical = formation icon, manager_quote = speech bubble,
  preview = reading icon, article = newspaper).

- "Score: 0.81" at the bottom of each card is a debug field. Should be
  hidden from the consumer view (the number is for the admin table).

- The "selected" highlight on one of the three 1X2 selections (e.g. Elche
  with purple gradient + green odds) has no reason to exist on a pre-match
  card. Either remove it or give it meaning ("recommended leg").

- Odds tiles are OK but could feel more tactile — touch targets, hover/tap
  state, and clearly tappable (they'll deep-link to the operator bet slip
  in Stage 5).

- No sense of recency / freshness. A 2-hour-old injury scoop should feel
  different from a 6-hour-old preview. Add subtle time-ago indicators
  (e.g. "2h ago" chip).

- No visible source attribution. The card was scouted from Sky Sports or
  BBC — that credibility should show up (small source line under the
  headline).

- The phone-shell mock around the feed made sense for a mockup. Now that
  we're embedding for real, we need an embedded-iframe layout variant:
  no phone chrome, edge-to-edge, responsive to container width.


5. DESIGN PROBLEMS TO FIX (admin table)
---------------------------------------

- Dense table, but already functional. What it needs:
  - Better affordances for reviewing a candidate: click a row to expand
    and see the full news summary + score breakdown + reason
  - Color-coded hook column (same palette as the public feed)
  - "≥thr" column is cryptic — should be a clearer pass/fail visual
  - Filters are serviceable but could be chips instead of selects
  - Counts-by-(hook × status) at the bottom could become a small chart
    (heatmap or stacked bar) at the top
  - Empty / loading states
  - An "open in feed" link per row — jump to the card as a user would see it


6. BRAND / AESTHETIC DIRECTION
------------------------------

- Dark mode default (sportsbook-native).
- Accent color: currently a blue-purple `#7c5cff` — open to changing, but
  the product needs a single strong accent.
- Typographic: clean, editorial. Headlines should feel like a sports site,
  not a banking app. Reference points: The Athletic, ESPN, BBC Sport.
- Data density: lean toward dense, not sparse. Users are scanning fast.
- Animation: subtle. New cards slide in from top. Odds tiles have a small
  tap/press animation. Nothing gimmicky.
- Themable via CSS variables — operators override accent color, font,
  radius, logo. Design system should be defined in terms of tokens.


7. KEY SCREENS / COMPONENTS TO DESIGN
-------------------------------------

a. FEED CARD (main atom). Variants:
   - Single-bet card (market has one selection to highlight)
   - Combo / accumulator card (multi-leg, total odds)
   - Bet Builder card (multi-selection same-event, total odds)
   - Live moment card (Stage 7 — goal scored, red card, suspension)
   - Price-move card (Stage 6 — line shifted)

b. HOOK-TYPE VISUAL SYSTEM (8 hooks)
   injury, team_news, transfer, manager_quote, tactical, preview, article,
   price_move, live_moment
   Needs: icon, color, pill style.

c. FEED PAGE (embedded mode)
   - Header: operator logo (if configured) + Pulse branding
   - Filter strip (chips): league | hook | sort
   - Feed (infinite scroll or paginated — your call)
   - Empty state ("no news-driven cards right now, check back in an hour")

d. ADMIN /CANDIDATES PAGE (desktop-dense)
   - Counts summary (chart/heatmap)
   - Filter bar
   - Table with expandable rows
   - Per-row "open in feed" link

e. DEEP-LINK CTA (future-proof)
   When a user taps a card / selection, we deep-link to the operator's bet
   slip. The CTA element needs to look clearly actionable and ideally carry
   the operator's brand (so users know they're being sent to THEIR operator).


8. CONSTRAINTS / NICE-TO-HAVES
------------------------------

- Mobile-first (375px width minimum)
- Tablet fine (iframe on operator desktop site — 600px–1200px typical embed)
- No per-card photography (we don't have rights to team images)
- Icons: open to bringing in a set like Lucide, Phosphor, or custom simple ones
- Font: system default is fine; if recommending a custom face, Inter is
  already loaded via the existing mock
- Language: English for MVP (Pulse is going into Apuesta Total, a Peruvian
  operator, whose primary language is Spanish — keep English cleanly
  translatable)


9. WHAT TO DELIVER
------------------

- Feed card system (all variants)
- Hook-type visual system
- Public feed page (mobile + iframe-desktop responsive)
- Admin /candidates redesign (desktop)
- CSS token strategy for operator theming

Out of scope for this pass:
- Motion / full animation spec
- iOS/Android native app design
- Marketing site


10. FEEDBACK I'VE GOTTEN FROM THE PRODUCT LEAD
----------------------------------------------

"We're trying to bring context to a market. The reason why someone bets —
that can be statistics, but I personally think it should be news-driven.
Statistics can be part of it, but the trigger should be something has
happened that makes this market interesting. Or this builder or this
combo."

Design should reflect this hierarchy. News on top. Market below. Stats as
evidence, not the headline.
